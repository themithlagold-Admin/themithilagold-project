Our Pond-to-Plate Process

How our organic makhana is processed traditionally

01

Wetland Harvesting

Harvested from deep clayey beds of freshwater ponds in Mithila, our farmers manually gather makhana seeds in muddy waters using traditional bamboo sieves.

02

Sun Drying & Grading

The collected black seeds are thoroughly cleaned, washed, and spread out under pure sunlight to dry. They are then graded by size for uniform heat absorption.

03

Roasting & Popping

Seeds are roasted in iron pans at precise temperatures. Harvesters then manually crack them with wooden mallets to instantly pop them into white gold flakes.

04

Polishing & Packing

The popped kernels are polished to remove remaining shells, inspected for crunchiness, sorted, and sealed immediately in airtight bags to preserve fresh quality.

Connecting Mithila's Bounty to the World

Growing up in the serene wetlands of Mithila, I always witnessed the hard work that goes into harvesting every single grain of makhana. Despite it being a nutrient-dense superfood, the local farmers rarely received the recognition or compensation they deserved.

Combining my background in computer applications (MCA) with my love for my homeland, I founded Mithila White Gold. Our Core Pillars

The principles guiding every pack we seal

🌿

100% Purity

We supply makhana exactly the way nature intended. Absolutely no chemical bleaching, artificial colors, or additives are used in our sorting or popping process.

Farmers First

By purchasing from us, you directly support fair-wage contracts and sustainable working conditions for the local pond harvesters of Mithila.
